Thiruvananthapuram is a major tourist centre, known for the Padmanabhaswamy Temple, the beaches of Kovalam and Varkala, the backwaters of Poovar and Anchuthengu and its Western Ghats tracts of Ponmudi and the Agastya Mala.While Trivandrum is the Capital of one of the most beautiful states of the country, it also is an impeccable tourist destination. The climate is favourable throughout the year for visiting this city that is home to a variety of physical features that land and water has to offer.Click here to know more about kollam

Kovalam

Kovalam is famous for its beaches, among the most pristine in India. Kovalam is extremely popular among westerners due to shallow waters and low tidal waves.

Ponmudi Hills

Ponmudi (the Golden Peak) also known as Kashmir of Kerala is a hill station in the Peringamala gramapanchayath of Trivandrum District of Kerala in India.

Napier Museum

The Napier Museum is an art and natural history museum situated in Thiruvananthapuram, India.

Varkala Tourist Destination

Famous for a temple that is believed to be around 2,000 years old, Varkala is also a religious site of pilgrimage for many Hindus.

Veli Tourist Villages

The Veli Tourist Village which lies where the Veli Lake meets the Arabian Sea provides for unique boating and picnicking opportunities.
